The **Blue/Brown Scheme** is a fantastic choice for a personal site because it feels "expensive" and sophisticated. It moves away from the "techy" vibe of the blue/yellow and into something more grounded and editorial.

Here is how you should assign these colors to create a professional, readable layout:

### 🟫 The Foundation (Earth Tones)
* **Deep Brown (#591F0A): The Typography & Footer**
    This is your "Anchor." Instead of using pure black for your text, use this deep brown. It’s much softer on the eyes and makes the whole site feel warmer. It also makes for a very solid, high-contrast **Footer** background.
* **Burnt Orange (#D65108): The Secondary Element**
    Use this for **secondary buttons**, **date stamps** on blog posts, or **blockquotes**. It bridges the gap between the brown and the bright amber.

### 🔵 The Contrast (Blues)
* **True Blue (#0267C1): The Brand Identity**
    Use this for your **Site Title/Logo** and **Main Navigation**. Blue and brown are complementary colors, so this blue will look very crisp against a white or cream background.
* **Cerulean Blue (#0075C4): The Interaction**
    Reserve this specifically for **Links** and **UI icons**. Since it’s slightly brighter than the primary blue, it naturally signals to the user that "this is a clickable element."

### 🟠 The "Pop" (Amber)
* **Amber Gold (#EFA00B): The Call to Action (CTA)**
    This is your "Look at me" color. Use it for your most important button (e.g., "Download Resume" or "Contact Me"). Because it is the only "warm" bright color in the set, the user's eye will be drawn to it instantly.

---

### Layout Mapping Example

| Element | Color Assignment |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Text** | Deep Brown (#591F0A) |
| **Main Action Button** | Amber Gold (#EFA00B) |
| **Navigation Links** | True Blue (#0267C1) |
| **Link Hovers** | Cerulean Blue (#0075C4) |
| **Borders / Dividers** | Burnt Orange (#D65108) (at low opacity) |

### Design Strategy: The "Academic" Look
To really lean into this palette's strength, try using a **Cream or Off-White background** (like `#FDFCF0`) instead of a harsh pure white. This makes the Deep Brown text look incredibly professional, almost like a high-end magazine or a classic library aesthetic.



---

**One quick check:** This palette has a lot of "warmth." Do you see your website having a lot of photos, or will it be mostly text and code snippets?
